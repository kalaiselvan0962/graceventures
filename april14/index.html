For Boo ❤️

"April 14 — A Memory I Still Hold"

You wore red that day, the sea learned your name before I dared to speak it.

The Story

That afternoon by the
edge of the sea.

Every year, April 14 arrives quietly — but it never arrives empty. It carries the warmth of that afternoon with it. The way the sky looked: soft and unhurried, stretched pale above Besant Nagar Beach. The sand still golden from a generous sun. And you, Boo — standing at the shore in that red dress, the breeze lifting the edges of it like the day was trying to hold you a little longer too.

— i — Wind off the warm sea, your red dress a quiet flame — I forgot to breathe.

We walked without a map and without any hurry. The church nearby — with its worn stone walls and their particular hush — felt like somewhere time had agreed to slow down for us. We stood inside for a while, not saying very much, because some moments don't need words. They just need presence. And you were so fully, beautifully present.

I don't think I understood that afternoon the way I understand it now. Sometimes distance is the only lens that shows you what you had. Today, a year later, I hold it carefully — like something made of light and thread — grateful that it happened, even if it cannot happen again.

— ii — Church bells, soft and slow, your laughter between the stones — God must have listened.

Missing you isn't loud. It doesn't announce itself. It arrives in small, specific things — the smell of salt air, the colour red catching the corner of my eye, a quiet afternoon that feels too quiet. You are not gone from me the way people think. You live in a different part of my day now — the softer part, the part that keeps the light on.

The Day, Unfolded

Every hour of that
April afternoon.

Morning · Setting Out 🌸

The Journey Begins

The city was already warm. We left before the afternoon could crowd itself with noise, finding our way toward the coast like it was the most natural direction in the world.

Two shadows on dust, the city shrinking behind us — sea calls, we follow.
Midday · Besant Nagar Beach 🌊

The Shore, and You

Waves arriving like small announcements. You stood at the edge where water meets sand — that in-between place — and the red of your dress against all that blue was something I will never stop seeing.

Shore swallows our feet, the sea takes and gives it back — like all things we love.
Afternoon · The Church ⛪

Stillness in the Stone

We slipped inside the old church near the beach, away from the brightness and the heat. Cool stone walls, muted light through coloured glass, and the kind of quiet you carry out with you long afterward.

Coloured light, old stone, your voice a folded prayer there — I still hold that hush.
Late Afternoon · The Golden Hour 🌅

The Moment Before Goodbye

The sun had softened into amber. We didn't rush. We let the day be what it wanted to be — unhurried and whole. I think we both knew, somewhere underneath the laughter, that this particular afternoon was something rare.

Golden light went slow, as if the sun too hated to leave you behind.

"Some people leave,
but the memories never do."

Boo, I am not writing this to reach you. I am writing it because some things deserve to be kept carefully — not locked away, but placed somewhere warm and lit. That April afternoon is one of them. You are one of them.

Thank you for that day by the sea. Thank you for the church, for the laughter, for the red dress and the silence that felt like company. Thank you for being someone worth missing this gently.

Not goodbye — just this: a page kept open for you, wherever you are.
